About Brunei

Negara Brunei Darussalam is a country located on the north-west coast of the island of Borneo and consists of 4 districts:

  • Kuala Belait
  • Tutong
  • Bandar Seri Begawan
  • Temburong

According to data collected in 2011, Brunei Darussalam has a population of 393,372. The majority of the population in Brunei are Malay with an estimation of 258, 465 people, followed by Chinese with an estimation of 40,534 people. Negara Brunei Darussalam consists of 7 ethnics which are Malay, Dusun, Kedayan, Bisaya, Belait, Kedayan and Murut. With the rest of the population consisting of minor groups.

EXPLORE BRUNEI

Brunei's Abode of Peace

In Brunei, its Islamic heritage takes centre stage and the state has built some iconic mosques around the country. Probably the most photographed religious landmark is the Omar ‘Ali Saifuddien mosque in Bandar Seri Begawan. This is usually a major highlight on inbound Islamic tours along with the famous mausoleum of the Sultan Bolkiah, the fifth Sultan of Brunei whose rule from 1485 made Brunei a powerhouse in the Malay archipelago, and the Islamic Calligraphy and Art Study Centre where priceless Islamic calligraphy art and artefacts are exhibited.

Luagan Lalak Forest Recreation Park

Luagan Lalak Recreation Park is home to an alluvial freshwater swamp known for its mesmerising beauty. The walkways extend out into the center of the swamp, as the mirror-like surface of the water reflects the open sky with all of its majesty. Each visit may be different too, as the water rises and falls with the seasons. In the dryer months, the water gives way to beautiful patches of sedges. It’s a great place for walks and provides a beautiful scenery.

Brunei's' art, culture and heritage

Brunei's history

Brunei is one of the world's oldest reigning monarchy. Brunei is home to centuries of royal heritage, with the current Sultan’s family line dating back over 600 years. Walk through time and discover relics from an ancient shipwreck, take a stroll around the surroundings of a royal tomb, and experience history all over again through numerous exhibitions and displays in museums.

Brunei's custom's and traditions

As a tight-knit, conservative community, Brunei is filled with warm politeness, evident in the way Bruneians speak, and dress. There are also hidden wonders in the vast expanses of the rainforests!

Islamic culture

Islam has been present in Brunei since the 10th century, and by the reign of Sultan Sharif Ali (Bruneis 3rd monarch), Brunei grew into an Islamic empire. With Islam as the state religion and the Malay Islamic Monarchy (MIB) concept as the national governing philosophy, Brunei flourished spiritually and through the people’s material well-being.

Brunei arts

Regarding Brunei's arts and crafts, Brunei is renowned for its boat making, silver smithing, bronze tooling, cloth weaving as well as mat and basket weaving. Brunei Darussalam also possesses natural capital by virtue of its rich tropical rainforests, as well as relics and other various artistic heritage which include Malay weaponry, wood carvings, traditional games, traditional musical instruments, Silat (the traditional art of self-defence) and decorative items for women.
